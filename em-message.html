Fire / Emergency Management / A Message from the Emergency Manager

Curry W. Mayer

Ms. Mayer has over 25 years of experience in emergency management and homeland security. Her work has included projects for a variety of large government organizations. She holds certifications in which she is also able to teach. Ms. Mayer is a Board member and an adjunct professor. To see her speak on BellevueNOW alongside Sophia Lopez, Emergency Mgmt Coordinator - Public Education, watch the video located below her letter.

The Office of Emergency Management’s (OEM) primary mission is to strengthen city resilience by incorporating a whole community approach in our daily operations. We do this internally by coordinating training and exercises of emergency operations with all City Departments and externally by providing all hazards public education and volunteer opportunities to business and residents of Bellevue.

OEM works with our Regional partners to develop and exercise plans that support the City’s ability to prepare for, respond to, mitigate, and recover from all types of hazards and threats. Our Public Education program provides training and materials on:

  • Personal Preparedness
  • All-Hazards Specific Safety and Preparedness
  • Map Your Neighborhood (A Washington State program that helps residents identify capabilities in their neighborhood that could be essential in a disaster or emergency)
  • Community Emergency Response Team (CERT) training
  • Volunteer Opportunities within the Fire Department include:
    • Citizen Corps Council
    • Bellevue Communications Support
    • Community Emergency Response Team (CERT)

If you cannot find what you are looking for, please feel free to contact us at 425-452-6807. We are happy to assist you.


Sincerely,

Curry W. Mayer
